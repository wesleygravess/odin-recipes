Dumplings

Home

Description

Tender, juicy pork wrapped in delicate dough, these dumplings are bursting with savory flavor. Seasoned with garlic, ginger, scallions, and a touch of soy sauce, each bite offers a perfect balance of richness and umami. Steamed, pan-fried, or boiled, they’re delicious on their own or dipped in a tangy vinegar-soy sauce.

Ingredients: Pork Dumplings (Makes ~30 dumplings)

For the filling:

  • lb (450g) ground pork (not too lean, 70–80% lean is best)
  • 11 cup napa cabbage, finely chopped
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper (or black pepper)

For the wrappers:

  • 30 round dumpling wrappers (store-bought or homemade)

For cooking:

  • 1–2 tbsp oil (for pan-frying)
  • 1/4 cup water (for steaming in pan-fry method)

Dipping Sauce (optional):

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Chili oil or garlic (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prep the filling:
    • In a large bowl, combine all filling ingredients.
    • Mix thoroughly (use your hands or a spoon) until sticky and well combined.
    • Let sit 15–30 minutes for flavors to meld (optional but recommended).

  2. Wrap the dumplings:
    • Place about 1 tablespoon of filling in the center of a dumpling wrapper.
    • Wet the edge with a little water.
    • Fold in half and pinch the edges to seal, pleating if desired.

  3. Cook the dumplings (choose one method):
    4. Pan-Fried (potstickers):
    • Heat 1 tbsp oil in a nonstick or cast iron skillet over medium heat.
    • Add dumplings flat side down. Fry until bottoms are golden brown (~2 min).
    • Add 1/4 cup water and cover immediately. Steam until water evaporates (~5 min).
    • Remove lid and cook 1 more min to crisp up bottoms again.
    Steamed:
    • Line a steamer basket with parchment or cabbage leaves.
    • Steam over boiling water for 8–10 minutes until pork is cooked through.
    Boiled:
    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
    • Gently add dumplings and stir.
    • When they float, boil another 2–3 minutes (total ~5–6 minutes).
    • Remove with a slotted spoon.

  4. Serve:
    • Serve hot with dipping sauce on the side.
