Description

Crispy on the outside, soft and chewy on the inside — this homemade pizza starts with a simple yeast dough and a rich, garlicky tomato sauce. Perfect for any topping you love, it’s a fun and satisfying way to make pizza night extra special. Whether you’re using a baking sheet or a pizza stone, this recipe delivers fresh, pizzeria-style flavor straight from your oven.

Ingredients: (Makes two 10–12" pizzas)

For the Dough:

  • 2¼ tsp (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1½ cups warm water (110°F/45°C)
  • 3½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Simple Tomato Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Optional Toppings:

  • Fresh mozzarella, shredded cheese, pepperoni, basil, mushrooms, etc.

Steps:

  1. Make the dough:
    • In a large bowl, dissolve sugar and yeast in warm water. Let sit 5–10 min until frothy.
    • Stir in flour, olive oil, and salt. Mix until a dough forms.
    • Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    • Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1–1.5 hours or until doubled in size.

  2. Make the sauce:
    • In a small pan, heat olive oil and sauté garlic until fragrant (~1 min).
    • Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15–20 min, stirring occasionally.

  3. Assemble:
    • Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). If using a pizza stone, preheat it too.
    • Punch down dough and divide in half. Stretch or roll into circles.
    • Top with sauce and desired toppings.
  4. Bake:
    • Transfer to a baking sheet or stone. Bake for 10–14 minutes or until crust is golden and cheese is bubbling.
